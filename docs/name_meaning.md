VREL — Valence-based Relational Engine for Language

"Feels like something from a 1970s AI lab — very SHRDLU-adjacent." - Claude

## Linguistic valence

the most legitimate reading

In linguistics, valence (or valency) refers to the number and type of arguments a verb requires. "Give" has a valence of 3: a giver, a recipient, a thing given. It's core to dependency grammar and directly relevant to NLU — understanding sentence structure means understanding valency.

So valence-based could legitimately mean: **the system parses and reasons about argument structure.**

## Chemical valence

the metaphorical reading

Atoms bond based on valence — how many connections they can form. Language works similarly: words bind to each other in structured ways. It's a nice metaphor for relational parsing.

## Sentiment valence

the weakest reading

In psychology/NLP, valence means positive/negative affect. Less relevant to what you're building unless sentiment is in scope.

## Jacobus Vrel

Jacobus Vrel was a mysterious 17th century Dutch Golden Age painter, which adds a whole layer.

* He painted **quiet domestic interiors** — intimate, everyday scenes. There's a nice parallel with NLU: finding structure and meaning in ordinary language, the mundane made legible.
* He's **largely unknown and enigmatic** — his identity was debated for a long time, works misattributed to Vermeer. A fitting patron for a library that wrestles with ambiguity.
